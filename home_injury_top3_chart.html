Top 3 Causes of Home Injuries

This chart shows a simple ranking (3 = highest) based on commonly cited U.S. public health sources. For precise counts by age, year, and place of occurrence, use CDC WISQARS or NSC Injury Facts.

Summary

  1. Falls — The most common home injury across ages, especially older adults and young children. Typical hotspots: stairs, bathrooms, and cluttered walkways. Prevention: clear trip hazards, add handrails and non-slip mats, improve lighting.
  2. Poisoning — Frequent in home settings (medications, cleaners, CO). It is the leading cause of preventable injury-related deaths in homes and communities. Prevention: lock medicines and chemicals, use original containers, install carbon monoxide alarms.
  3. Burns & Scalds — Common in kitchens and bathrooms (hot liquids, stovetops, appliances). Prevention: set water heater to ≤120°F/49°C, turn pot handles inward, use oven mitts, keep children away from hot surfaces.

Sources: National Safety Council — Injury Facts (Home & Community, 2023); CDC — WISQARS Nonfatal Injury Data.
